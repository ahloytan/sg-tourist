Dos & Don'ts in Singapore

✅ Dos

Getting Around & Eating
  • 🚆 Do use public transport; it's efficient and easy to navigate.
    • Mass Rapid Transit (MRT) map - here
    • Best Value: Use Apple/Google Pay directly at the tapout. Cheapest way for short stays. No extra card fee.
    • Alternative: Get an EZ-Link card (S$10, includes S$5 non-refundable card cost and S$5 stored value for travel. For longer stays, get a Tourist Pass for unlimited rides if you're hitting 6+ spots a day.
  • 💵 Do carry cash for hawker centres and markets.
    • List of Hawker Centres - here
    • Note: Most hawker stalls open throughout the day, but there are some that operate for the morning/lunch vs dinner crowd only. Do check the timings before you go!

General
  • 🏃 While on escalators, do stand on the left and walk on the right.
  • ☀️ You are encouraged to bring sunblock and a hat for protection from the sun.
  • 💧 Bring a water bottle for hydration as it can get quite hot and humid. Some hawker centres and venues are equipped with water refill stations.
  • 💨 Do bring a portable fan if possible!
  • ☕️ It is a legal requirement to return your tray and crockery to designated stations after your meal.
  • 🗑️ Do keep public places clean and dispose of litter properly - even a small candy wrapper can earn a fine.
  • 🧍‍♂️ When visiting a hawker centre, a tissue packet left on a table serves as a reservation marker.
Before entering Singapore
  • Fill in arrival card here: ICA Arrival Card
  • Purchase E-SIM beforehand. Or you can purchase one at the airport upon arrival here

🚫 Don'ts

Behaviour & Regulations
  • Don't chew gum or bring large quantities into the country (it's banned).
  • Don't smoke in non-designated areas; heavy fines apply.
  • Don't litter - you could receive an on-the-spot fine.
  • Don't be disrespectful towards officers, rangers, or public staff.
Transit & Photography
  • Don't eat or drink on public transit (MRT, buses).
  • Don't take photos in places clearly marked "no photography" (e.g., certain museums).
  • Drone flying regulations here

📌 Note

Unfortunately, if you are thinking about renting a car in Singapore using car-sharing services like GetGo and TribeCar, you are unable to do so without a Singapore driver's licence. If you really have to, you may consider Hertz/Avis/Enterprise, but that may set you back SGD $200+/day
